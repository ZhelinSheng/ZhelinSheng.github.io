About

I am currently an undergraduate student at University of Rochester, expected to receive BS in Applied Mathematics and BA in Computer Science in 2024.

Research interests: High dimentional probability, harmonic analysis, and applied data science

At early stage of my undergraduate career I assisted George Ferguson in teaching machine learning course and started doing research in our math and data science department. There I am fortunate to be advised by Alex Iosevich. We have discovered high dimention reduction tools involving fractal pheonomena both in theory and real life. Now we turn our attentions toward a universal approaximation for the situation under functions that the "typtical approximator", Neural networks, cannot perform well on, including but not limited on the high dimentional functions with fractal pattern.

For the memory of my first partisipated paper, I'm keenly interested in make our Discrete-S tool practical in real life sooner or later
